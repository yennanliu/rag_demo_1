### 1. The Standard RAG Prompt Template

This structure uses clear delimiters (`---` or `###`) to prevent the model from getting confused between the "custom info" and the "user query."

```markdown
### System:
You are a helpful AI assistant. Use the provided context to answer the user's question accurately. 
If the information is not in the context, state that you don't know rather than making up an answer.

### Context:
---
{retrieved_info}
---

### User Question:
{user_query}

### Response:
```

---

### 2. A Concrete Example (Tony Q Demo)

If your RAG system pulls the specific snippet about Tony Q, the final prompt sent to the model would look like this:

> **### System:**
> You are a professional assistant. Answer based strictly on the provided context.
>
> **### Context:**
> ---
> Employee Profile: Tony Q. 
> Role: Senior Software Engineer (SDE).
> Experience: 10+ years in distributed systems and backend architecture.
> Current Focus: AI-driven development tools and high-concurrency ticket systems.
> ---
>
> **### User Question:**
> Does Tony Q have experience with scaling systems?
>
> **### Response:**
> Yes, Tony Q is a Senior Software Engineer with over 10 years of experience specifically in distributed systems and backend architecture, which involves scaling systems. He is currently focused on high-concurrency ticket systems.

### 3. Pro-Tips for "Invisible" RAG

To make your PoC feel more like a real product, consider these prompt engineering tweaks:

* **Handle "Out of Bounds" Questions:** Add a line like: *"If the context doesn't mention the person, tell the user to check the internal directory."*
* **Persona Matching:** Since you're using Qwen (which is great at following instructions), you can add: *"Answer in a concise, technical tone suitable for a lead engineer."*
* **Multi-Language Support:** If your info is in English but your user asks in Traditional Chinese, add: *"Please provide the final response in Traditional Chinese."*
